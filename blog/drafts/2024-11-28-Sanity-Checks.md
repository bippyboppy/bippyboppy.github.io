# Confidence Calibration
Being underconfident and overconfident are both incredibly harmful, if you're underconfident you won't go as hard as you otherwise should, the lack of certainty or probability will impact the choice you make and the risk you're willing to take, being overconfident has much more obvious negative impacts 

The way to address this is through keeping a log locally or publically (mine can be found [here](https://bippyboppy.github.io/docs/predictions)) You basically log your predictions in terms of confidence (I use a range of 55%-95% in grades of 10%) then see how correct each prediction is at each level of confidence, 55% confidence should be 55% correct, etc etc. 

For a more practical insight into how I do this I use an emacs org file [link](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/bippyboppy/bippyboppy.github.io/refs/heads/main/docs/predictions.org) then I swap out TODO/DONE to INCORRECT/CORRECT meaning each time a state change occurs it will automatically keep a % of correct for me, then every now and again I export it to my site
