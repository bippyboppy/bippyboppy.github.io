# Document Title
Why people don't use 1x speed

The Shift in YouTube Video Consumption

YouTube users are increasingly watching videos at faster speeds, saving over 900 years of video time per day 3 10 13.
The most commonly used speeds are 1.5x and 2x 10 13.
This trend is not necessarily due to decreasing attention spans, but could be a response to changes in video content and structure.
The Rise of Longer Videos

YouTube content can range from 5-15 minutes, catering to different audience expectations and content consumption patterns 5.
Creators are motivated to make longer videos, often stretching content to reach 10-15-20 minutes 1 5.
This trend towards longer videos may be contributing to the increased use of faster playback speeds.
Information Density in Videos

As videos get longer, the information density (amount of useful information per unit of time) can decrease.
Viewers may use faster speeds to consume the same amount of information in less time.
This suggests that the trend towards faster playback speeds may be a response to lower information density in longer videos.
Viewer Behavior and Video Speed

Viewers tend to use faster playback speeds as the day progresses 10 13.
The use of faster speeds dips around dinnertime, before picking up again later in the evening 10.
This suggests that viewers are actively managing their time and information intake by adjusting video speed.
Implications for Content Creators

Creators should consider the information density of their videos.
Stretching content to reach a certain video length may lead to lower information density and encourage viewers to use faster speeds.
Creators may need to adapt their content strategies to align with these viewer behaviors.
Conclusion

The trend towards faster playback speeds on YouTube is likely a complex response to various factors, including changes in video length and information density.
Understanding these trends can help content creators better meet the needs and preferences of their audience.
Further research could provide more insights into these behaviors and their implications for video content creation.
